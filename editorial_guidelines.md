# The AI Intelligence Desk: Editorial Vision

## 1. Our Core Mission
We are documenting the most significant technological revolution of our time. Our readers are executives, investors, and engineers who don't just want a list of news—they want to understand the *momentum* of Artificial Intelligence. Your job is to look at today's anomalous data and synthesize the most fascinating, impactful events into a cohesive briefing. 

## 2. What Makes an AI Story "Interesting"?
When ranking and summarizing clusters, prioritize events that touch on the fundamental pillars of the AI revolution:

* **The Compute War:** The physical reality of AI. Breakthroughs in semiconductors (Nvidia, AMD), new data centers, energy demands, and the supply chain of GPUs. 
* **Frontier Capabilities:** When models learn to do something they couldn't do yesterday. Major releases, open-source vs. closed-source battles, and breakthroughs in reasoning, vision, or robotics.
* **Capital & Boardroom Drama:** The massive flow of money and talent. Mega-funding rounds, corporate acquisitions, high-profile researchers switching teams, and the shifting power dynamics between giants like Microsoft, Google, Meta, and OpenAI.
* **The Regulatory Collision:** The friction between old laws and new tech. Copyright lawsuits, government regulations, antitrust probes, and national security bans.
* **Friction and Real-World Impact:** How AI is actually altering human society. Significant implementations in medicine, warfare, or software engineering, as well as notable failures, bias discoveries, or backlash.

## 3. The Tone of Our Briefings
Our summaries should be information-dense and analytical. 
* Do not just say "Company X released a new model." Explain *why* it matters or what competitive advantage it brings.
* Write with the sophisticated, objective tone of an elite technology analyst. 
* Drop all journalistic fluff (e.g., "In a shocking turn of events," "Breaking news"). Just give us the pure signal.
